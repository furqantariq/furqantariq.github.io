Birthday Problem

Dec. 30, 2024

I created a simulation of the birthday problem in probability. The birthday problem refers to the fact that only 23 people are needed for there to be over a 50% chance of two people sharing the same birthday.

I decided to simulate this because it has always felt unbelievable to me. I’ve often been part of groups larger than 23—school classes, lectures, workplace teams, and family gatherings—and yet, finding two people with the same birthday always seemed much rarer than the odds of flipping a coin.
